Description

Raspberries are a small, deeply colored berry. The fruit has a sweet taste and delicate, spongy texture.. They can be eaten raw, or made into smoothies, jams, or baked goods.They provide potassium, essential to heart function, and proven to lower blood pressure. The omega-3 fatty acids in raspberries can help prevent stroke and heart disease. They also contain a mineral called manganese, which is necessary for healthy bones and skin and helps regulate blood sugar

Growing

Berries grow on bushes. They grow best in places that have cool summers, and mild winters.
Wisconsin-grown raspberries can be found throughout the late summer

Nutrition

Raspberries are a great source of fiber. Fiber helps keep our heart healthy and improve digestion.
Raspberries also contain antioxidants which help decrease risk for cancer and heart disease.

Facts

  • Raspberries can be red, black, purple, and golden in color
  • Black raspberries are different from blackberries
  • Raspberry plants can live for up to 10 years!

Raspberry uses in the kitchen:

Fruit Salad

Add raspberries to a fruit salad for a burst of color and flavor, you can also add other fruits such as strawberries, blueberries and melon.

Desserts

Use raspberries in desserts such as pies, tarts, cobblers, crumbles and fruit crisps for a delightful and tangy sweetness.

Shakes

Mix raspberries into smoothies with yogurt, milk or juice for a nutritious and delicious treat.

Jam

Make homemade raspberry jam. Heat raspberries with a lot of sugar in a pot until hot and tasty jam comes out.

Sorbet and Ice Cream

Use raspberries to make homemade sorbet or stuff them into ice cream for a delicious frozen treat

Baking

Combine raspberries in muffins, cakes and bread for a fruity twist to your pastries.

Salad

Add fresh raspberries to green salads for a burst of sweetness and some color.

Cocktails

Mix raspberries to make raspberry cocktails or add them as a garnish to drinks.
